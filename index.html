Welcome to my studio!
"...Music education is essential to being a human. For me teaching piano is a beautiful journey to discover life through the sounds of such a magical musical instrument as piano. In my students I value their unique personalities, musical talent and dedication to work. I am committed to helping my students acquire skills necessary to express their musical ideas, feelings as well as demonstrate their competency within any musical environment..."

Russian pianist, Elena Akopova, moved to the US
in 2007 to continue her piano studies at the San Francisco Conservatory of Music.

She was offered a place at Mack McCray’s studio and soon established herself in the Bay Area as an accompanist, a chamber music collaborator and a private piano/music theory teacher.

Within the framework of Master Degree Program in Piano Performance, Elena was admitted to the highly competitive Chamber Music Program at San Francisco Conservatory and was trained by Paul Hersh, Yoshikazu Nagai.

In the academic year 2008-2009 she held a position of SFCM Piano Department Assistant.

Ms. Akopova received many awards in her native Russia. She is a laureate of Astrakhan National Piano Competition and Sochi Chamber Music Competition. She won also the International Competition of Young Performers in Jyväskylä, Finland.

Elena enjoys teaching the piano and has an extensive teaching experience. For two years she was a member of faculty at Krasnodar Music College as well as Krasnodar School of the Arts. Now, Elena is an active member of the Palo Alto Branch of the Music Teachers Association of California. She continues her passion teaching in the Bay Area.

Elena has taught at Golden Key Piano School (Berkeley), Community Music Center (SF), Aria Music Academy (SF), SF Friends School (SF), Pacific Music Academy (San Jose). Elena’s provides many opportunities for her students to participate in numerous musical events. Her students regularly compete at USOMC, MLOC, Russian Music Festival and win top prizes. Many of her students demonstrate excellent results at ABRSM and CM tests.

Teaching adults at any level is Elena’s special gift she enjoys sharing with local professionals in Silicon Valley.

Ms. Akopova graduated from Krasnodar Conservatory of Music in 2007 and studied with Natalia Ziger and Nelly Mezglumova. Being a student, she took part in numerous master classes, including those conducted by Jose Ribera, Aleksey Skavronsky and Tat’yana Zagorovskaya.

Elena Akopova is a musician of broad and diverse interests. She made recordings for several documentary movies and visual presentations, including soundtracks for the movie “Maggots and Men” directed by Oakie Treadwell.

While living in SF, Elena worked as an intern at San Francisco Performances. She made a big contribution to several projects, including Marino Formenti’s solo piano recital/lecture. Elena supplemented his unique performance with high quality visual and sound materials illustrating the concepts presented by Mr. Formenti.

Apart her love to music, Elena is deeply interested in Assyrian art, language and culture and is passionate in knowing better her ancient roots.
